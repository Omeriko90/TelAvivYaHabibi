Tel Aviv, Israel’s business and cultural capital, is the center of the country’s largest metropolitan area. Liberal and wealthy, this beachfront city is all about lifestyle. Restaurants and cafes abound and are full at all hours, making some wonder whether anyone here works. The gay scene here is flourishing, and goes far beyond hot nightclubs; same-sex couples strolling with babies and strollers are a common sight. It’s known among Israelis as “the bubble,” or alternately “the Country of Tel Aviv” – disparagingly or fondly, depending on whom you’re talking to. One thing they’ll all agree on is that visitors will find no shortage of ways to keep busy.
